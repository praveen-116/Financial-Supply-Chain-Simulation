# Financial-Supply-Chain-Simulation
Simulation-based analysis of traditional and smart contract-enabled supply chain models using Excel and Google Colab.


## Project Overview

This project presents a simulation-based comparative analysis of traditional and smart contract-enabled supply chain coordination models. The study was developed to evaluate operational efficiency, financial coordination, and decision-making performance under varying demand conditions.

The project integrates operational supply chain simulation, financial performance analysis, and smart contract-based revenue-sharing mechanisms to investigate how coordination strategies impact inventory management, profitability, cash flow, and overall supply chain efficiency.

This repository includes implementations developed using **Microsoft Excel**, **Python (Google Colab)**, and **smart contract-based financial coordination logic**.

---

## Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are:

- Simulate a traditional multi-stage supply chain system
- Develop a smart contract-enabled revenue-sharing model
- Compare operational and financial performance between both systems
- Analyze supply chain coordination effectiveness
- Evaluate business KPIs under uncertain demand conditions
- Validate consistency between Excel and Python simulation approaches

## Models Implemented

### 1. Traditional Excel Supply Chain Model

An Excel-based supply chain simulation model developed to analyze operational behavior across multiple supply chain entities.

#### Key Features:
- Demand generation and order fulfillment
- Inventory tracking
- Lost sales analysis
- Fill rate evaluation
- Operational cost estimation
- Profit analysis

`01_Traditional_Excel_Model/`

---

### 2. Python-Based Supply Chain Simulation

A Python implementation developed using Google Colab to model supply chain operations and validate system consistency.

#### Key Features:
- Dynamic inventory simulation
- Demand uncertainty handling
- Multi-stage supply chain modeling
- Service level evaluation
- Cost and profitability assessment
- Comparative operational analysis

📂 Location:
`02_Python_Supply_Chain_Model/`

---

### 3. Smart Contract Revenue Sharing Model

A smart contract-enabled financial coordination model developed to analyze revenue-sharing mechanisms among supply chain members.

The model evaluates how financial coordination improves supply chain collaboration while maintaining operational consistency.

#### Financial Metrics Evaluated:
- Revenue distribution
- Profit allocation
- Cash flow analysis
- Cash-to-Cash (C2C) Cycle Time
- Effective Cash Realization Time
- Financial coordination efficiency

📂 Location:
`03_Smart_Contract_Revenue_Sharing_Model/`

## Results & Findings

The comparative analysis demonstrated:

- Traditional and smart contract-enabled models produced consistent operational outcomes.
- Smart contract-based revenue-sharing improved financial coordination across supply chain entities.
- Improvements were observed in cash flow efficiency and effective cash realization time.
- Inventory behavior and service levels remained operationally stable across implementations.
- Validation across Excel and Python implementations confirmed model consistency and reliability.
